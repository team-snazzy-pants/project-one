Research

Our business model is based on zillow.com. Below are some strategic decisions we made based on our research.

We debated a lot how to display price range. We debated having a single field for max price. Not many people distinguish a min price range but after reviewing our research we opted to use a min and max.

We adjusted our property types to match those of our research, eliminating commercial property. Our focus is entirely on residential properties.

We added a rating attribute to the StaffMember entity. This will serve as a query parameter for clients seeking an agent.

We modeled our rental attributes on Zillo's.

Participation Constraints

  • Property has a total relationship with Branch for the handles relationship because we assume that every property is handled by a branch.
  • Branch has a partial participation with Property for the handles relation because not all branches necessarily handle properties.
  • Client has total relationship with Branch for the has relationship because we assume all clients have a home branch.
  • Branch has a partial participation with Client for the has relationship because a new or under construction branch may not have any clients.
  • Property has a total relationship for the has relation because we assume all properties are owned by a client.
  • Client has partial participation between property for the has relationship because there can be clients who do not have a property.
  • Both StaffMember and Property have partial participation in the listedBy relationship because we assume there could be staff members who don't list properties (like a teller) and also properties that aren't currently listed (like a brand new property or a property under renovations).
  • There is total relationship for StaffMembers in the worksAt relation because all staff members work at a branch. There is partial participation for Branch in the worksAt relation because a branch could be new or under construction and not have any StaffMembers.
  • There is a total relationship for advertised because all ads are for a property.
  • PropertyStats has a total relationship with property because every property has property stats and all property stats have their property.
  • OpenHouse has a total relationship with property because every open house is for a specific property
  • Offer has a total relationship with property because every offer is for a property.
  • Listing has a total relationship with property because every listing is for a property.

Business Constraints

Every entity (Client, Branch, StaffMember, Property, Ad, PropertyStats, OpenHouse, Offer and Listing) must have a unique integer-based ID
  • The ID is used to create relationships between entities and relationships as well as for identification purposes
There is a finite number of branches
  • Oversaturating any given location with branches would reduce the demand of any one branch
A Branch cannot exist without a location
  • This particular business model operates with the branch as the central hub for all StaffMembers of the branch. Unlike a web-based business this branch cannot conduct business without a physical location.
Branches can have more than one Property
  • Though somewhat obvious this is an assumption of the business practice

Business Constraints not covered by Design

    There is no relationship between StaffMember or Client
  • If a StaffMember is also a Client there is no mapping to indicate this relationship
  • Relationships only exist between Property and Client or Client and Listing Agent

After some debate, we opted to not model a relationship between staff member and client to keep from getting bogged down in minutia and to keep the model from becoming unnecessarily complex. Our model was already becoming quite complex without this relationship.

Relational Diagram

see draw.io for relationalDiagram
  • We modeled N:M relationships with the relationship relation / cross-reference option. We created a separate relation and used the primary keys of the entities modeled as foreign keys.
  • We modeled inheritance with StaffMember and its child classes sellingAgent and listingAgent. We created separate relations for the superclass and the two subclasses.
  • We also modeled inheritance with client and its child classes buyer, renter and owner. We created separate relations for the superclass and the three subclasses.
  • We did not have any ternary relationships.
  • We mapped multi-attribute attributes using a separate relation. Each has a foreign key of its entity and a combined primary key of the foreign key plus an attribute of the multi-valued entity.
  • There were no constraints that were represented in ER design, but could not be mapped to the relational design.
  • One design choice we made was to use inheritance to model StaffMember. This makes it easy to quickly understand the soldBy and listedBy relationships for StaffMember. Information gained by this design choice was commission attribute on each child class. This could be modeled more simply without inheritance, but it would be harder to quickly understand.
  • Another design choice we made was to use inheritance to model Client. This makes it easy to quickly understand the buyer, renter and owner subclasses. These could be modelled using attributes but this method is more clear
